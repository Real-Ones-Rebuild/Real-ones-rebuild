How I Got Clean in Prison

There was a time when I didn’t think freedom was real. Not just physical freedom, but freedom from the grip of addiction, guilt, and silence.

I walked into prison broken. I was addicted to heroin, cocaine, pills — anything that numbed the pain of the things I hadn’t faced.

But one day, something shifted. I looked at the man I was becoming and I hated it. I didn’t want that to be my story. So I got clean.

What I Learned

Getting clean isn’t about willpower alone. It’s about confronting the pain that got you there. It’s about choosing growth over comfort.

And I’ll say this: I’m not proud of everything I did to survive, but I’m proud of the man who made it out of that place stronger, wiser, and sober.

We rebuild together.
