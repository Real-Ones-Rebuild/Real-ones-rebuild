Fighting for My Son in Family Court

By Marquis Owens

They say the system is supposed to be fair.
But from the moment I stepped into that courtroom, I felt like I was already losing.

Not because I was unfit. Not because I was a danger. But because of my past — because of who I used to be. A man who fought addiction. A man who served time. A man they thought didn’t deserve to be a father.

But they forgot something: I rebuilt.

I became a certified peer specialist. I earned a psychology degree. I got clean. I stayed clean. I trained to recognize abuse, de-escalate crisis, and help people transform.
And most importantly — I never stopped showing up for my son.

🏛️ Court Doesn’t See the Full Story

In court, people look at paper.
But paper doesn’t show the full picture.

It doesn’t show the hours I sacrificed.
The pain of missed birthdays.
The strength it took to face a judge without a lawyer — because I believed my voice mattered.
It doesn’t show the way I light up when I see my son smile, even for just 3 supervised hours.

It doesn’t show the fight in my heart.

🧍🏾‍♂️ I’m Not Just Fighting for Custody

I’m fighting for his childhood.
I’m fighting for my presence in his life.
I’m fighting to make sure my son doesn’t grow up thinking his father didn’t care.

Because I know what that feels like. I know what it’s like to wonder why your dad didn’t show up.
And I refuse to let my son carry that wound.

This isn’t about control. It’s about connection.
It’s about being there for first steps, scraped knees, bedtime prayers, and real conversations.
It’s about breaking the cycle I was born into — and building something better.

✊🏾 Real Fathers Don’t Walk Away

We fight.

Even when the odds feel stacked.
Even when the system moves slow.
Even when they try to define us by our past instead of our progress.

I am not the man I was.
I’m the father I was always meant to become.

🕊️ If You’re Fighting Too…

Don’t give up.

You may feel alone.
You may feel silenced.
But your consistency is louder than any accusation.

Keep documenting. Keep praying. Keep growing.
And never stop showing up — in court, in life, in love.

Because we rebuild together.
