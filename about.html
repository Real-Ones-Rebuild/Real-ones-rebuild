About Marquis Owens

I’m Marquis Owens. I’m a father, a certified peer specialist, a psychologist, a man who spent four years behind bars, and a man who’s now fully free — in mind, in heart, and in purpose.

I come from pain. I know what it’s like to be fatherless, to be judged, to be addicted, to be overlooked, and to be locked away. But I also know what it’s like to overcome all of it — not because I’m perfect, but because I refused to stay broken.

I struggled with addiction for years — pills, heroin, and cocaine were the escape. I went to prison. I traded food for Suboxone. I broke my mother’s heart. And for a while, I thought maybe that’s all life had for me.

But God didn’t leave me there.

In prison, I got clean. Not just physically — I got clean spiritually, mentally, emotionally. I looked at the man I was and chose to become someone different. I let go of the lies that said I’d never be a father, never be respected, never matter again. And I started rebuilding — one prayer, one truth, one rep, one tear at a time.

Today, I am the man I needed when I was a child.

I earned my degree in psychology. I became a certified peer specialist through the Pennsylvania Certification Board. I now work to help others through trauma, addiction, and mental health battles. I know what they feel because I lived it — and made it out.
