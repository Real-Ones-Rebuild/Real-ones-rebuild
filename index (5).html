My Story — by Marquis Owens

I wasn’t supposed to make it.

Not according to the system. Not according to the people who counted me out. Not even according to the old version of me — the one who got lost in addiction, ended up behind bars, and thought maybe that was all life had for him.

But I rebuilt.

I was addicted to pills, heroin, and cocaine. I lost years of my life to pain I never learned how to talk about. I went to prison, broke, angry, and numb — but inside those walls, something started to shift. I got clean. I got real. And I started finding out who I actually was.

Since then, I’ve earned my degree in psychology. I became a certified peer specialist in Pennsylvania. I show up every day — not just for myself, but for my son, Holden, who became the reason I breathe. I’ve already stood before the judge in my custody trial. Now, I’m waiting on a decision — praying the court sees the man I’ve become, not the mistakes I’ve made. Because my son deserves to grow up with a father who’s present, committed, and full of purpose.

That’s what HoldenStrong means.

It’s not just a brand — it’s a promise. A promise to my son that I’ll never stop showing up. A promise to myself that I’ll never return to the life that broke me. And a promise to others that we can rebuild, even when the foundation was shattered.

This platform — Real Ones Rebuild — is bigger than me. It’s for every man who’s been counted out. Every father trying to break generational cycles. Every person who fell hard and still found the strength to rise.

I’m not perfect. But I’m proof.

We rebuild together.
